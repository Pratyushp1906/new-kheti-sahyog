Welcome to Kheti Sahyog

We are on a journey to revolutionalize farming in our country

Kheti Sahyog

The affordability of expensive farming tools is a significant issue for numerous impoverished farmers. These tools are critical for enhancing agricultural output, but their high price can act as a hindrance for small-scale farmers. Consequently, many are compelled to stick to less efficient, traditional methods. To tackle this problem, governments and non-governmental organizations (NGOs) can provide subsidies or low-interest loans to farmers for buying farming tools. Additionally, introducing alternative, cost-effective technologies could also help in enhancing productivity without putting a financial burden on farmers.

How are we doing this?

Our website serves as an online platform for farmers to conveniently buy, sell or rent agricultural tools by interacting with sellers. The platform aims to facilitate easy communication and transactions between farmers and sellers. Our price comparison feature allows farmers to compare prices of the products being offered, ensuring they get the best possible price for their needs. This feature not only benefits farmers, but also helps sellers to remain competitive in the market. Our goal is to make agricultural transactions more accessible, efficient and cost-effective for all parties involved. By using our platform, farmers can easily find the tools they need and sellers can reach out to a wider audience. Overall, we aim to simplify the agricultural tool market and promote growth in the industry.

Why Kheti Sahyog?

According to the World Bank, about 80% of the world's farmers are small-scale farmers who rely on subsistence farming to survive. Many of these farmers are located in developing countries, where poverty is widespread, and access to capital and resources is limited.

A significant issue facing poor farmers is their inability to afford essential farming tools. Farming tools such as plows, tractors, and fertilizers are crucial in achieving efficient and productive farming. However, these tools are often expensive, making them inaccessible to farmers who lack the financial means to purchase them. Without these tools, poor farmers are forced to rely on traditional farming methods, which are labor-intensive and yield lower crop production. As a result, they struggle to make ends meet and provide for their families. The lack of farming tools also leads to a vicious cycle of poverty, where poor crop yields result in limited income and an inability to purchase better equipment. This further perpetuates their poverty and dependence on subsistence farming. Moreover, the inability to afford farming tools exacerbates the problem of food insecurity in impoverished areas. Insufficient food production results in food shortages and inadequate nutrition, which negatively impacts the health and well-being of communities.
